  3. {% translate "Processing of personal data and categories of processed data" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} When using SERIMA, Personal data is processed. {% endblocktranslate %}
    2. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Specifically, this concerns the following categories of data: User identification and contact details (surname, first name, email address associated with the account), information relating to the roles performed within an Operator and professional contact details (e.g. information relating to the identity of the members of the management body, the CISO, the IT security manager), data related to log files as described in Article 5 below. {% endblocktranslate %}
  4. {% translate "Purposes and legal bases for processing" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Personal data is processed in accordance with the GDPR. The User’s data is recorded and processed by the relevant Regulator solely for the purposes of managing the procedures for which it is responsible. Data processing is carried out in the context of making SERIMA available to Operators to enable them to fulfil their legal notification obligations and to carry out and transmit incident notifications, risk analyses and declarations relating to their security measures to the Regulators. {% endblocktranslate %}
    2. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} The provision of SERIMA contributes to the fulfilment of the legal tasks conferred on Regulators and Observers in accordance with the Rules of Law. The Processing of personal data by these Regulators and Observers is therefore based on Article 6(1)(e) of the GDPR (processing necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest). {% endblocktranslate %}
  5. {% translate "Log files" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Data relating to the User's activity on SERIMA (log files recording downloads, features used, date and time of connections and notifications) is collected for the sole purpose of making this data available to the relevant Controller of personal data or to the judicial authorities in the following limited cases: {% endblocktranslate %}
      1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} resolution of security incidents related to SERIMA in order to guarantee the security of SERIMA; {% endblocktranslate %}
      2. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} the establishment, exercise or defence of a legal right, in the context of judicial, administrative or extrajudicial proceedings. {% endblocktranslate %}
  6. {% translate "Recipients of the processed data " %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Personal data communicated in a notification sent to a Regulator on the basis of the applicable legal provisions via the SERIMA platform is accessible to persons designated to access it as "Regulator Users" or "Regulator Administrators" within the meaning of the Terms of Use, as well as to the Operator who made the notification. {% endblocktranslate %}
    2. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Depending on the applicable Rules of Law, notifications sent to a Regulator may be transferred by the latter to other Regulators or Observers. {% endblocktranslate %}
    3. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} The list of SERIMA platform Users, as well as the information relating to Users as described in point 3 above, is accessible to all Regulators, in accordance with the Terms of Use. {% endblocktranslate %}
    4. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} In the context of processing related to the SERIMA platform, the National Cybersecurity Competence Centre (NC3 - 122 rue Adolphe Fischer, L-1521 Luxembourg), which is part of the Luxembourg House of Cybersecurity (LHC), acts as a Processor on behalf of the Regulators. {% endblocktranslate %}
    5. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} The categories of Personal data mentioned in point 3 above are accessible to designated agents within the NC3 responsible for the proper functioning and maintenance of the SERIMA platform, in particular in the context of technical support to Users and to the extent that such access is necessary to provide this support. {% endblocktranslate %}
  7. {% translate "Operator's representations and warranties" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} By providing Personal data under its sole responsibility, the Operator declares and warrants that it has obtained and will obtain all legally required consents and authorisations from all persons concerned by the use, processing and transfer of Personal data. {% endblocktranslate %}
  8. {% translate "Technical and organisational measures" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Technical and organisational measures are in place to ensure a high level of data security on SERIMA. These security measures are tested and updated regularly. {% endblocktranslate %}
  9. {% translate "Retention period" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} The Personal data required to create an account on SERIMA is retained for the entire duration of the account's existence. It is permanently deleted when the account in question is deleted. {% endblocktranslate %}
    2. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Data related to log files as described in point 5 above may not be retained for more than two years. However, this period may be extended when necessary for the purposes of establishing, exercising or defending a legal claim. {% endblocktranslate %}
    3. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Notifications sent to Regulators via SERIMA are subject to an administrative retention period of ten years. At the end of this ten-year period, notifications may be retained beyond this period for archiving purposes in the public interest, following the retention rules determined by the Regulators in accordance with the Law of 17 August 2018 on archiving. {% endblocktranslate %}
  10. {% translate "Rights of data subjects" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Data subjects may access data concerning them and obtain a copy thereof (Article 15 of the GDPR), obtain the rectification of inaccurate or incomplete data (Article 16 of the GDPR), object to the Processing of their data (Article 21 of the GDPR), obtain the deletion of such data under the conditions set out in Article 17 of the GDPR and the restriction of processing under the conditions set out in Article 18 of the same regulation. Data subjects may exercise their rights by contacting the data protection officer of the competent Regulator. {% endblocktranslate %}
    2. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} With regard to the ILR, data subjects may exercise their rights by contacting the ILR's data protection officer: https://www.ilr.lu/informations-legales/protection-des-donnees/. {% endblocktranslate %}
    3. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} With regard to the CSSF, data subjects may exercise their rights by contacting the CSSF's data protection officer: dpo@cssf.lu. {% endblocktranslate %}
    4. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} With regard to the HCPN, data subjects may exercise their rights by contacting the HCPN's data protection officer: info@cgpd.etat.lu. {% endblocktranslate %}
    5. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} With regard to the CNPD, data subjects may exercise their rights by contacting the CNPD's data protection officer: dpo@cnpd.lu. {% endblocktranslate %}
  11. {% translate "Complaints" %}
    1. {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Individuals who believe that the processing of data via SERIMA constitutes a violation of the GDPR may lodge a complaint with the CNPD using the form available on its website. {% endblocktranslate %}
